    Mitigation Measures

    Conservation efforts focus on mitigating habitat loss by establishing and maintaining protected areas and wildlife corridors. Reforestation projects aim to restore degraded habitats, providing essential living spaces for leopards. Conservation institutions, such as the Sri Lanka Wildlife Conservation Society, actively work on habitat restoration projects and engage with local communities to promote sustainable land-use practices.

      Mitigation Measures

      To combat illegal wildlife trade, Sri Lanka has implemented stringent anti-poaching measures and increased law enforcement efforts. Conservation organizations like the Wildlife and Nature Protection Society of Sri Lanka work tirelessly to raise awareness about the consequences of wildlife trade and collaborate with law enforcement agencies to combat this illicit activity. Public education campaigns emphasize the importance of protecting leopards and the broader ecosystem from the threats posed by illegal wildlife trade.
